# Grammar V2 Decision Gate

Renaming the structural hierarchy does not by itself create GRAMMAR-V2.
GRAMMAR-V1 remains an immutable historical release interpreted under its own
published ATOMS-V1 vocabulary.

A future GRAMMAR-V2 name is justified only if, after Corpus V3 is frozen, at
least one substantive condition is true:

- grammar induction is rerun from corrected Corpus V3 data;
- canonical atom boundaries become an explicit modeled level;
- the train/test protocol or frozen rule inventory changes; or
- a new preregistered validation produces a separately frozen model.

If only labels are translated through the nomenclature crosswalk and all data,
rules, and metrics remain identical, the result is a compatibility view, not a
new grammar release.

Any future GRAMMAR-V2 must cite PARTICLES-V1, CORPUS-V3, its induction command,
its frozen inputs, and a comparison against GRAMMAR-V1 without rewriting the
historical package.
